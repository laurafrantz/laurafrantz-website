We returned to [Woods Edge farm](https://laurafrantz.com//wordpress/2010/11/woods-edge.html) for our third annual bonfire on Saturday. [Angelina](http://www.pathkeepers.org/#!board-of-directors/cee5)‘s non-profit [hosted a camp](https://indiancountrytodaymedianetwork.com/2013/08/19/nonprofit-gives-native-youth-summer-remember-150880) for Native American youth this summer, and many of her campers returned for the day. So many sounds: Johnny Cash coming out of the barn speakers, horses neighing, a leaf blower making [a giant pile for jumping](http://www.flickr.com/photos/laurafrantz/8125956912/), laughter, native flute.

I spent time in the chilly upper pasture trying to get a good shot of the boy horses grazing. The only way I could keep them near me was to sing, so I made up the most ridiculous, operatic songs about horses and belted them while setting up my shots. Just when I thought I had the perfect picture, the horses were spooked by a squirrel. A SQUIRREL. So much for the power of opera.
