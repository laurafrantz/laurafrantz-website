![dock](image-01.jpg)

Tomorrow evening, the kids and I will be at John’s grandmother’s place, watching the sun set over **[the**](http://blog.laurafrantz.com/2008/06/delaware-may-2008.html) **[bay**](http://blog.laurafrantz.com/2008/10/this-time-before-snows.html). This is probably the last summer we’ll be able to spend time at the beach house: John’s grandmother passed away in December and the house most likely will be sold at the end of the season.

Ten random traditions we’ll be observing over the next ten days:

*::* **[Morning coffee**](http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3243/2959705249_01cceb0561_o.jpg) on the dock\
:: Saying **[goodbye to the sun**](http://farm1.static.flickr.com/148/415783662_5d9a8d6821.jpg) on the dock\
:: Midnight visits to the dock (can you tell we like the dock?)\
:: A large pie with onions, sweet peppers and mushrooms from ***[Nicola Pizza***](http://www.nicolapizza.com/) in Rehoboth\
:: **[Fisher’s caramel corn**](http://www.fisherspopcorn.com/) in Bethany (can you tell we like to eat?)\
:: Book browsing at **[Bethany Beach Books\
**](http://www.bethanybeachbooks.com/):: Letterboxing at **[James Farm**](http://www.inlandbays.org/cib_pm/james_farm.php), which is within walking distance of the house via road or squishy bay bottom at low-tide\
:: \$1 toy shopping at ***[Fun For All***](http://www.funforalltoys.com/about_us/about_us.html) in Rehoboth\
:: Clamming at low tide, followed by our famous clams casino (again with the eating)\
:: Wading to the far shore of the cove to hunt for dessicated baby horseshoe crabs (not for eating)

I’ll miss you while I’m away, but I promise to send you a postcard. Or a **[picture**](http://www.flickr.com/photos/laurafrantz/sets/72157603505321889/). Better yet, hop in the car and drive on over. We’ll be waiting at the end of the dock.
