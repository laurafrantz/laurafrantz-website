[My part of the world is being hit hard by the Orange Death](http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2010/04/05/AR2010040503721.html). Thick deposits of Tang-colored powder cover my car, the grill, the white patio table and chairs I ~~picked from the neighbor’s trash~~ freecycled last week in another spasm of [accidental eco-hipness](http://blog.laurafrantz.com/2009/01/staying-home.html). My throat is raw and my blood is most likely 50% pollen at this point, so I’ve been staying inside quite a bit lately, pretending to be a writer.

I’ve been coordinating with editors on some submissions that are already on retainer. I’ve been sending queries, which makes me feel like a trollop begging for fifteen minutes in the backseat. But mostly I’ve been starting things. I’ve started essays, letters, chapters, projects, collaborations, stuff that could be good if I’d only focus on it. The starting is easy, but the finishing is dreadfully slow, and I am a procrastinator from waaaaay back. In fact, right after typing that last sentence, I wasted five minutes in front of the window, watching the helicopters fall from the maples and thinking that later today my little ones will drop handfuls of them from the second floor deck to watch them twirl to the ground. Then I stared at my cuffs and wished my favorite khakis from Old Navy weren’t falling apart quite yet, and began devising a scheme to patch them with an old bed sheet I thrifted from the Salvation Army. Then I stared at the used coffee grounds for a while and thought about how much they look like [Polaroid SX-70 manipulations I’ve seen around flickr lately](http://www.flickr.com/photos/kevindean/4330803333/).

People, I am in trouble. Simply sitting back down at the computer does not end the procrastination insanity. The Internet is like a giant window that looks out on a giant stand of maple trees that are all shedding their helicopters at once. My hope is that if I pass on these distractions to you, I’ll be free of them forever and might be able to put two sentences together today. So here goes, a (partial) list of stuff I am looking at instead of working, but don’t say I didn’t warn you:

\+ [Analogue Daydreams](http://vimeo.com/9385947), a documentary-style amateur video explaining the art of Holga photography

\+ A [video interview](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=psl7u1v2DpU) with Karen Laborie, illustrator and friend. I spent two summers at her home in southeast France when I was a teenager, and now she’s gone and growed up on me. The interview is in French, so you might want to warm up your brain a little before watching. But isn’t she CUTE??

\+ Brian Ferry (no, not of Roxy Music) takes the [best pictures](http://bferry.wordpress.com/) and makes some [great mixes on 8tracks](http://8tracks.com/bferry), too

\+ My kids and I have been laughing for two days over all the franglais on the [McDonald’s France](http://www.mcdonalds.fr/) website. “Le cheeseburger” hits their funny bone especially hard for some reason, maybe it’s because of the thick French accent (and goofy face) I use when I pronounce it for them

\+ I’ve been half-heartedly monitoring the mass-hysteria resulting from [ABC’s story on unschooling](http://abcnews.go.com/GMA/Parenting/unschooling-homeschooling-book-tests-classes/story?id=10410867). I’m amused by the hoopla and grateful for having dodged that particular educational bullet

\+ Listening to [WQJZ’s live webcast](http://www.delmarvabroadcasting.com/player/?st=Jazz), complete with commercials for Delmarva businesses, makes me feel like I’m at the beach house in Delaware, making avocado and tomato sandwiches for lunch as we wait for low tide

OK. Back to work, if one can in fact call compulsively checking email “work”.
